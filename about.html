What is Tennis, Really?

THE VIBE

Tennis is essentially high-speed chess with a massive physical component. Every shot requires quick thinking, smart positioning, and anticipation of your opponent’s next move. It’s not just about hitting the ball hard—it’s about strategy, timing, and staying calm under pressure while constantly moving across the court.

At the beginner level, the game might feel fast and chaotic, but over time you’ll start to recognize patterns and develop your own playing style.

Tennis can be both competitive and social, making it a sport you can enjoy casually or take seriously as you improve.

THE RACKET

Use a large head-size racket (100+) for a bigger sweet spot. This makes it easier to connect cleanly with the ball, especially for beginners. A larger sweet spot reduces mistakes and helps you build confidence as you develop your technique and control.

Lighter rackets are also easier to swing and control, which is ideal when you’re just starting out.

As you improve, you may choose rackets that offer more precision or power depending on your play style.

THE FOOTWEAR

Wear proper tennis shoes for side-to-side movement. Unlike running shoes, tennis shoes are designed to support quick changes in direction, prevent slipping, and protect your ankles during lateral movement on the court.

Good footwear not only improves your performance but also helps prevent injuries.

Playing in the wrong shoes can make movement harder and increase the risk of slipping or losing balance.

THE GOAL

Hit the ball over the net and inside the lines. The aim is to place your shots in a way that your opponent cannot return them successfully. Consistency, accuracy, and smart placement matter just as much as power.

Winning points often comes down to forcing your opponent into making mistakes rather than hitting perfect shots.

As you improve, you’ll start thinking about strategy—like where to aim, when to attack, and when to play it safe.
