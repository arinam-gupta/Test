TRAIN SMART, NOT HARD

CONSISTENCY FIRST

Don’t try to hit hard. Focus on getting the ball over the net every time — that alone will beat most beginners.

A steady, controlled shot is much more valuable than a powerful one that goes out. Try aiming for the middle of the court to reduce mistakes.

Building consistency early helps you develop confidence and longer rallies.

FOOTWORK

Good tennis is all about movement. Stay light on your feet and always adjust before hitting the ball.

Small steps (called adjustment steps) help you get into the right position instead of reaching for the ball.

Try to return to a ready position after every shot so you're prepared for the next one.

GRIP

Use a comfortable grip. Don’t squeeze too hard — relaxed hands give you better control.

Holding the racket too tightly can reduce flexibility and make your shots less accurate.

As you improve, you can experiment with different grips for forehands, backhands, and serves.

PATIENCE

Improvement takes time. Focus on small improvements every session.

It’s normal to make mistakes while learning — every player goes through it.

Track small wins, like longer rallies or better serves, to stay motivated.

SIMPLE DRILLS

WALL PRACTICE

Hit against a wall repeatedly to improve control and consistency.

This is one of the easiest ways to practice alone and get lots of repetitions quickly.

Try counting how many shots you can hit in a row without missing to track your progress.

RALLY WITH FRIEND

Try to keep the ball going as long as possible instead of trying to win points.

Focus on cooperation rather than competition — the goal is to build consistency together.

Start close to the net, then slowly move back as your control improves.

TARGET PRACTICE

Aim for specific areas of the court to improve accuracy.

You can use cones, markers, or even just imagine zones on the court.

Practicing accuracy helps you place the ball where your opponent isn’t, making it harder for them to return.

SERVE PRACTICE

Practice serves slowly first — consistency matters more than power.

Focus on getting the ball into the correct service box before trying to add speed.

A reliable serve helps you start points confidently and reduces double faults.

WATCH BEGINNER DRILLS

Watching drills in action can help you understand proper technique and movement.

Try to copy the rhythm, positioning, and control shown in the video during your own practice sessions.
