THE RULES (SIMPLIFIED)

SCORING

Tennis scoring goes 15 → 30 → 40 → game. It looks weird at first, but you’ll get used to it quickly once you play.

If both players reach 40, it becomes deuce. From there, a player must win two points in a row: the first gives advantage, and the next wins the game.

Example: If you win the first point, the score is 15–0. If your opponent wins the next point, it becomes 15–15, and so on.

Don’t worry about memorizing everything right away — most players learn scoring naturally by playing a few games.

WINNING A GAME

You need at least 4 points AND be 2 points ahead to win a game. If it's 40-40, that's called deuce.

After deuce, one player gets whats called an "advantage". If they win the next point, they win the game. If they lose it, the score returns to deuce.

Games can sometimes last a long time if players keep reaching deuce repeatedly — this is completely normal.

SETS

First to 6 games wins a set (must win by 2). Matches are usually best of 3 sets for beginners.

For example, a set score could be 6–4 or 7–5. If players reach 6–6, a tiebreak is usually played.

In a tiebreak, points are counted normally (1, 2, 3...) instead of 15, 30, 40. The first player to 7 points (with a 2-point lead) wins the set.

Matches can feel long, but they’re broken into sets and games, which makes them easier to follow.

SERVING

You must serve diagonally into the service box. If you miss twice, you lose the point (double fault).

Each point starts with a serve. Players take turns serving after each game.

Your feet must stay behind the baseline when serving, and you must hit the ball before it bounces.

A strong and consistent serve can give you a big advantage in a match.

HOW POINTS ARE WON

OUT BALLS

If your opponent hits the ball outside the lines, you win the point.

Lines are considered in, so even a tiny part of the ball touching the line counts as a good shot.

Learning to aim deep into the court (near the lines) can make it harder for your opponent to return the ball.

NET

If the ball hits the net and doesn’t go over, the point is lost.

On a serve, if the ball touches the net but still lands in the correct box, it's called a let and you replay the serve.

During rallies, even a slight touch of the net that stops the ball will end the point immediately.

MISSES

If your opponent fails to return the ball before it bounces twice, you win the point.

You can also win a point if your opponent hits the ball into the net or out of bounds.

Players are allowed to hit the ball in the air (a volley), which is commonly used near the net.

SIMPLE RULE

Just keep the ball in play longer than your opponent — that’s honestly the easiest way to think about it.

Many beginners try to hit too hard and make mistakes. Focus on control, accuracy, and consistency first.

As you improve, you can start adding power, spin, and strategy to your shots.

WATCH IT IN ACTION

Watching real gameplay helps you understand how scoring, serving, and rallies all connect during a match.

Try to notice when points end, how players win them, and how the score changes after each rally.
